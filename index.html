Market Environment 10 min

Global Context:

Look for gaps (up/down/flat), magnitude of move, and volume. Large gaps may fill, small gaps may extend.
Risk-on (green across regions) vs risk-off (red/mixed). Strong directional moves often continue into US session.
Rising DXY/yields = headwind for stocks. Falling = tailwind. Note magnitude of moves and trend changes.
Under 15 = low vol, 15-25 = normal, over 25 = high vol. Affects position sizing and strategy selection.

News & Events:

Focus on high-impact events (CPI, NFP, Fed decisions). Note consensus vs actual for surprises that move markets.
Hawkish tone (rates higher) = bearish stocks. Dovish tone (rates lower/pause) = bullish stocks. Watch for policy shifts.
Wars, elections, trade issues = risk-off. Major earnings beats/misses from large cap stocks affect overall sentiment.
Tech leading = growth/risk-on. Utilities/REITs leading = defensive/risk-off. Energy up = inflation concerns.

Market Character:

Trending = momentum strategies work better. Range-bound = mean reversion/fade strategies preferred. Look at recent daily structure.
Price in value = balanced/range-bound likely. Price outside value = trending/imbalanced conditions possible.
Large overnight moves often lead to quieter days. Small overnight moves may lead to bigger intraday ranges.
Advance/decline ratios, new highs/lows, put/call ratios. Divergences between price and breadth signal potential reversals.

Red Flags - Consider Sitting Out

  • Major pending news that could cause chaotic price action
  • Feeling emotional, unfocused, or mentally off
  • Levels too clustered or unclear for good risk/reward
  • Extremely low volume or holiday conditions
  • Fighting yesterday's losses instead of focusing on today
